Norman H. Gunn

Father of Forensic Podiatry

Norman H. Gunn is recognized as a pioneer of forensic podiatry, a discipline that examines footprints, footwear impressions, and gait patterns for forensic identification. Gunn demonstrated that feet and walking patterns exhibit individual characteristics that can be scientifically analyzed. His work established methodologies for footprint measurement, pressure distribution analysis, and gait assessment. These techniques allowed investigators to associate footprints and footwear impressions with individuals beyond simple shoe size comparison. Forensic podiatry is applied in cases involving barefoot impressions, surveillance footage analysis, and disputed identity. Gunn emphasized that podiatric evidence must be interpreted within anatomical and biomechanical contexts. Modern forensic podiatry incorporates digital imaging, pressure-sensitive platforms, and motion analysis systems.
