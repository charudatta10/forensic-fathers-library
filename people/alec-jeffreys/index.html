Alec Jeffreys

Father of DNA Fingerprinting

Sir Alec John Jeffreys (born 1950) is the inventor of DNA fingerprinting, one of the most transformative technologies in forensic science and criminal justice. Working at the University of Leicester in the 1980s, Jeffreys discovered that certain regions of DNA exhibit high variability between individuals while remaining stable within families. In 1984, Jeffreys developed techniques to visualize these variable regions, producing distinctive banding patterns unique to each individual. This breakthrough provided an unprecedented level of accuracy for human identification, surpassing all previous biometric and biological methods. DNA fingerprinting was first applied in a criminal case in 1986 in the United Kingdom, where it both exonerated an innocent suspect and identified the true perpetrator of two murders.
